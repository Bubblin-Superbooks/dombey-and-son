Mr Toots sat silent: folding and refolding the newspaper as small as possible upon his knee.

‘And Sol Gills,’ said the Captain, gazing at the fire, ‘poor nevyless old Sol, where are you got to! you was left in charge of me; his last words was, “Take care of my Uncle!” What came over you, Sol, when you went and gave the go-bye to Ned Cuttle; and what am I to put in my accounts that he’s a looking down upon, respecting you!
