‘Major,’ says Mr Dombey, ‘I assure you that I am really obliged to you. I had no idea of checking your too partial friendship.’

‘Not too partial, Sir!’ exclaims the choleric Major. ‘Dombey, I deny it.’

‘Your friendship I will say then,’ pursues Mr Dombey, ‘on any account. Nor can I forget, Major, on such an occasion as the present, how much I am indebted to it.’

‘Dombey,’ says the Major, with appropriate action, ‘that is the hand of Joseph Bagstock: of plain old Joey B., Sir, if you like that better! That is the hand, of which His Royal Highness the late Duke of York, did me the honour to observe, Sir, to His Royal Highness the late Duke of Kent, that it was the hand of Josh: a rough and tough, and possibly an up-to-snuff, old vagabond.
