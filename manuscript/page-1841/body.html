On abandoning his watch for that purpose, he could not help calling once, rapturously, through the keyhole, ‘Drownded. Ain’t he, pretty?’—or, when he got downstairs, making another trial at that verse of Lovely Peg. But it stuck in his throat somehow, and he could make nothing of it; so he went to bed, and dreamed that old Sol Gills was married to Mrs MacStinger, and kept prisoner by that lady in a secret chamber on a short allowance of victuals.
